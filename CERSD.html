3MT Competition

About Event


Three Minute Thesis (3MT) Â is a research communication competition developed by The University of Queensland (UQ) which challenges research higher degree students to present a compelling oration on their thesis and its significance in just three minutes in language appropriate to a non- specialist audience.


Eligibility


All Civil Engineering students are eligible


Rules

  1. Presentations are limited to 3 minutes and competitors exceeding 3 minutes are disqualified.
  2. Presentations are considered to have commenced when a presenter starts their presentation through speech (timing does not include the 3MT title slide and commences from when the competitor starts speaking, not the start of the video).
  3. Videos must meet the following criteria:
    1. Filmed on the horizontal;
    2. Filmed on a plain background;
    3. Filmed from a static position;
    4. Filmed from one camera angle;
    5. Contain a 3MT title slide;
    6. Contain a 3MT PowerPoint slide (top right corner/right side)
  4. A single static slide is permitted in the presentation (no slide transitions, animations or âmovementâ of any description). This can be visible continuously, or âcut toâ (as many times as you like) for a maximum of 1 minute or submitted via email if not included in the presentation.
  5. The 3 minute audio must be continuous â no sound edits or breaks.
  6. No additional props (e.g. costumes, musical instruments, laboratory equipment and animated backgrounds) are permitted within the recording.
  7. Presentations are to be spoken word (e.g. no poems, raps or songs).
  8. No additional electronic media (e.g. sound and video files) are permitted within the video recording.
  9. The decision of the adjudicating panel is final.
  10. Submissions via video format (only video link provided to Event Coordinators). Files sent in other formats will not be accepted.
  11. Entries submitted for final adjudication to Wildcard or University Final are to be submitted from the School/ Faculty/Institute 3MT Event Coordinator. Competitors should not submit their videos directly to 3MT.

Please note: competitors *will not* be judged on video/ recording quality or editing capabilities (optional inclusions). Judging will focus on the presentation, ability to communicate research to a non-specialist audience, and 3MT PowerPoint slide.



Judging Criteria


Comprehension and Content

  1. Did the presentation provide an understanding of the background and significance to the research question being addressed, while explaining terminology and avoiding jargon?
  2. Did the presentation clearly describe the impact and/or results of the research, including conclusions and outcomes?
  3. Did the presentation follow a clear and logical sequence?
  4. Was the thesis topic, research significance, results/impact and outcomes communicated in language appropriate to a non-specialist audience?
  5. Did the presenter spend adequate time on each element of their presentation - or did they elaborate for too long on one aspect or was the presentation rushed?

Engagement and Communication

  1. Did the oration make the audience want to know more?
  2. Was the presenter careful not to trivialise or generalise their research?
  3. Did the presenter convey enthusiasm for their research?
  4. Did the presenter capture and maintain their audience's attention?
  5. Did the speaker have sufficient stage presence, eye contact and vocal range; maintain a steady pace, and have a confident stance?
  6. Did the PowerPoint slide enhance the presentation - was it clear, legible, and concise?

Awards


The Top three entries will be awarded Best 3MT awards along with attractive prize money.
The winners names will be showcased at the official FaceBookpage, Magazine and other social media platforms of IITH Civil Engineering.

Technical Quiz

Eligibility


All Civil Engineering students are eligible


Rules

  1. Quiz will be conducted online mode only (Google form/Google meet).
  2. Quiz will be conducted for each specialization individually.
  3. Each team shall consist of 2 students/1 student - (One from B.Tech/ M.Tech and/or One from Ph.D.)
  4. The decision on the quiz results from selection committee will be final and will not be subjected to any change.
  5. The questions shall be in the form of multiple choice, True / False statement, Specific answer question etc.
  6. Replacement of any participant of a team is not allowed after registration.
  7. Each round results will be declared through a Technical quiz WhatsApp group.

Round-1


Preliminary Round - On basic research topics. (Through google form)

  1. Consist of 20 or 30 questions- Each question carries 1 mark (No negative marks)-Duration 10mins or 15minutes. Google form should be submitted on time.
  2. Only 10 teams would be selected to advance round (Depending on number of entries).
  3. In case of tie between 2 or more teams, further 5 questions would be asked orally for advance round selection.

Round-2


Advance round - On Advance research topics (Through google meet).

  1. Each team would be asked 2 question each and each question carries 10 marks (No negative marks).
  2. If a team cannot answer the question, they can pass the question and then the question would be forwarded to the next team.
  3. Answering time is only 20 seconds.
  4. Only top 5 teams would be selected for the Rapid fire round.

Round-3


Rapid Fire Round - Can be on experiments or computational techniques etc.

  1. Each team will be asked as many questions as possible one after another in one minute of time.
  2. 10 marks for the correct answer and negative 5 marks for the wrong answer.
  3. If a team cannot answer the question, they can say pass for the next question.
  4. The question will not be forwarded to the next team.
  5. Top 3 team will be selected for the Final-Audio Video round.

Round-4


Final Round - Audio Video round-(?)

  1. A Video clip or audio clip or Image (for 2 mins.) will be projected on the screen.
  2. 12 questions would be asked to all the teams.
  3. Whoever unmute mic first will get the chance to answer. (Like buzzer round)
  4. 10 marks for the correct answer and negative 5 marks for the wrong answer.
  5. Time is limited to 20 seconds to answer the question.
  6. No passing is allowed to the next team.

Awards


The Top three entries will be awarded Winner of Technical quiz along with attractive prize money.
The winners names will be showcased at the official FaceBookpage, Magazine and other social media platforms of IITH Civil Engineering.
